The Rasi chart (Rasi Chakram, ராசி சட்டம், రాశి చక్రం) is the primary birth chart in Vedic (Jyotish) astrology. Unlike Western tropical astrology, the Vedic chart uses the sidereal zodiac — corrected by the Lahiri ayanamsa (currently ~23°51'). This means all planet positions are shifted by about 24 degrees compared to a Western chart. The result is a far more astronomically precise chart that aligns with actual star positions.

South Indian vs North Indian Rasi Chart

South Indian Style
  • Fixed rasi positions in 4×4 grid
  • Mesha always 2nd cell, top row
  • Lagna marked with diagonal lines
  • Used in Tamil Nadu, Kerala, AP, Karnataka
  • Easy to compare charts at a glance
North Indian Style
  • Lagna always at top, rasis rotate
  • Diamond pattern with 4 triangles
  • House numbers shown by rasi number
  • Used in North India
  • Lagna position immediately visible

FAQs — Rasi Chart Online

What is a Rasi chart?
A Rasi chart (Rasi Chakram) is the main birth chart in Vedic astrology. It shows which of 12 zodiac signs (rasis) each of the 9 planets occupied at birth.
How is South Indian Rasi chart different from North Indian?
In the South Indian chart, the rasis are fixed to specific cells in a 4×4 grid. In the North Indian chart, the Lagna is always at the top and rasis rotate around the diamond.
Which ayanamsa is best for Rasi chart?
Lahiri (Chitrapaksha) ayanamsa is the standard accepted by the Government of India and used by most jyotishis.
What is the difference between Sun sign and Moon sign (Rasi)?
In Vedic astrology, your "rasi" is your Moon sign — the zodiac sign the Moon was in at birth. This differs from the Western Sun sign. The Rasi determines your nakshatra and the start of your dasha period.
