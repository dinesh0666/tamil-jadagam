What is Nalla Neram?

Nalla Neram (நல்ல நேரம்) literally means "good time" in Tamil. It refers to the most auspicious time window of the day — traditionally calculated using the Hora system (planetary hours), specifically the hours ruled by Jupiter (Guru) and the Moon (Gowri). These are considered the most benefic windows for starting important activities.

Nalla Neram vs Gowri Neram

✅ Nalla Neram

Ruled by Jupiter (Guru) — the most auspicious hora. Best for: business launches, important meetings, signing contracts, travel, seeking favours, buying property, new jobs.

🌸 Gowri Neram

Ruled by Moon (Chandra/Gowri) — second most auspicious. Best for: creative work, women's activities, cooking, family matters, emotional conversations, prayers.

How Nalla Neram is Calculated

The Hora system divides the day into 24 hours, each ruled by one of the 7 classical planets in the Chaldean order: Sun, Venus, Mercury, Moon, Saturn, Jupiter, Mars. The first hora of each day is ruled by that day's planet (e.g., Sunday → Sun, Monday → Moon), and subsequent hours follow the Chaldean sequence.

Nalla Neram = the hora window when Jupiter rules.
Gowri Neram = the hora window when the Moon rules.

Since sunrise shifts by city and season, the exact start times vary — which is why our Panchangam calculator computes it with actual NOAA sunrise data for each city.

  • Start a new business or sign important documents
  • Begin a new job or project
  • Start long-distance travel
  • Buy gold, property, or vehicles
  • Visit temples for important prayers
  • Start medication or treatment courses
  • Conduct naming ceremonies, tonsure (tonsure)
