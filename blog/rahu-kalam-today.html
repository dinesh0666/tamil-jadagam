Rahu Kalam (இராகு காலம்) is a daily 90-minute period considered inauspicious in Tamil and South Indian astrology. Named after the shadow planet Rahu, this time window is traditionally avoided for starting new ventures, travel, business agreements, surgery, or weddings.

Rahu Kalam Weekly Chart

Rahu Kalam occurs once daily. The day is divided into 8 equal parts from sunrise (approximately 6:00 AM) to sunset (approximately 6:00 PM). Rahu Kalam occupies one of these 8 slots depending on the day of the week:

What to Avoid During Rahu Kalam

  • Starting a new business or signing contracts
  • Important travel (especially long-distance)
  • Surgeries or medical procedures (non-emergency)
  • Wedding ceremonies and engagement rituals
  • Purchasing property, vehicles, or gold
  • Beginning new projects or studies

Nalla Neram — Auspicious Times

While Rahu Kalam is inauspicious, Nalla Neram (நல்ல நேரம்) identifies the most auspicious window of the day based on your birth Nakshatra. Our Panchangam page calculates both Rahu Kalam and Nalla Neram for today.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I eat during Rahu Kalam?
Yes — Rahu Kalam only applies to beginning new activities. Eating, sleeping, and routine tasks are fine.
What if an emergency happens during Rahu Kalam?
Emergencies override astrological considerations. These timings are traditional guidelines, not absolute rules.
Does Rahu Kalam change with daylight saving time?
Yes — the calculation is based on local sunrise and sunset times, which change with the season and geography.
