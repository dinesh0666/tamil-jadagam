In Tamil Vedic astrology, Nakshatra compatibility (நட்சத்திர பொருத்தம்) is the primary method for assessing marriage compatibility. Unlike Western sun-sign matching, Tamil astrology compares the birth star (Nakshatra) of both partners across ten specific dimensions called Poruthams.

What is a Nakshatra?

The sky is divided into 27 Nakshatras (lunar mansions), each spanning 13°20'. Your Nakshatra is determined by the Moon's position at the exact time of your birth. Each Nakshatra has a ruling planet, an associated deity, a Gana (nature), and a Yoni (animal symbol) — all of which feed into the compatibility calculation.

The 10 Poruthams Explained

1. Dinam (தினம்) — Daily Harmony · 3 points
Counts from the groom's Nakshatra to the bride's. If the count is divisible by 3, 5, or 7, it indicates health and longevity. This is considered one of the most important Poruthams.
2. Ganam (கணம்) — Nature Compatibility · 6 points
Each Nakshatra belongs to one of three Ganas: Deva (divine), Manushya (human), or Rakshasa (fierce). Deva-Deva and Manushya-Manushya are ideal. Deva-Manushya is acceptable. Rakshasa combinations need careful consideration.
3. Mahendram (மகேந்திரம்) — Progeny & Prosperity · 5 points
The count from groom to bride should fall on the 4th, 7th, 10th, 13th, 16th, 19th, 22nd, or 25th star. Indicates wealth, children, and a long life together.
4. Sthree Dheerga (ஸ்திரி தீர்க்க) — Happiness · 7 points
The count from the bride's Nakshatra to the groom's must exceed 7 stars. Ensures happiness, fortune, and a prosperous household for the bride.
5. Yoni (யோனி) — Physical Compatibility · 4 points
Each Nakshatra is assigned an animal symbol (Yoni). Compatible animal pairs ensure physical harmony. The same Yoni is ideal; certain pairs like horse-horse or cow-cow are excellent.
6. Rasi Porutham (ராசி பொருத்தம்) — Rasi Compatibility · 3 points
The Rasi (Moon sign) of the bride and groom is compared. Certain Rasi combinations (1-7, 1-6, etc.) are favourable. This affects the overall emotional chemistry.
7. Rasi Athipathi (ராசி அதிபதி) — Lord Compatibility · 2 points
The planetary lords of both Rasis should be friendly or neutral. Enemy lords reduce compatibility. This determines the deeper life-path alignment.
8. Rajju (ரஜ்ஜு) — Critical Dosha · Non-negotiable
The most critical Porutham. Nakshatras are grouped into five Rajju circles. Both partners must NOT belong to the same Rajju group — if they do, it is believed to cause the death of one partner. Rajju mismatch is considered a non-negotiable deal-breaker in traditional Tamil astrology.
9. Vedha (வேதம்) — Affliction Check · Non-negotiable
Certain Nakshatra pairs are considered mutually afflicting (e.g., Ashwini-Jyeshtha, Bharani-Anuradha). If both partners' Nakshatras form a Vedha pair, it indicates obstacles and is traditionally avoided.
10. Vashyam (வசியம்) — Attraction · 2 points
Based on the Rasi, this checks mutual attraction and influence between the couple. Certain Rasi pairs naturally draw each other — like Leo-Aries or Scorpio-Cancer.

Guna Score — What Do the Numbers Mean?

The Guna Milan system (used in North Indian Kundali matching) assigns points to each Porutham. The total out of 36 is the Guna score:

  • 30–36: Excellent — highly auspicious match
  • 24–29: Good — recommended for marriage
  • 18–23: Average — acceptable with remedies
  • Below 18: Not recommended without astrologer consultation
