What is a Nakshatra?

A Nakshatra (நட்சத்திரம்) is one of 27 lunar mansions in Vedic astrology. The sky is divided into 27 equal segments of 13°20' each, and the Moon transits through one of these every ~24 hours. The Nakshatra the Moon occupies on a given day is the day's Nakshatra — used for Panchangam, auspicious timing, and birth chart analysis.

Your birth Nakshatra is the Nakshatra the Moon was in at the exact moment you were born — and it's the most important single indicator in Tamil astrology, determining your Dasha sequence, personality, and compatibility.

Nakshatra & Daily Life

Knowing today's Nakshatra helps plan auspicious activities. Some key uses:

  • Weddings & events — certain Nakshatras (Rohini, Hasta, Mrigashira, Chitra, Swati, Anuradha, Uttara Ashadha, Uttara Bhadrapada, Revati) are considered muhurta Nakshatras — ideal for auspicious beginnings
  • Travel — avoid travel when the Moon is in your Janma Nakshatra (birth star) or the 8th Nakshatra from it
  • Medicine / Surgery — avoid procedures when the Moon afflicts the body part ruled by today's Nakshatra
  • Business — starting a business on Pushya, Rohini, or Uttara Phalguni Nakshatra is considered auspicious
