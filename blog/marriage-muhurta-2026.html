Choosing the right marriage muhurta (திருமண முகூர்த்தம்) is one of the most important decisions in Tamil astrology. A marriage conducted during an auspicious muhurta — when the Nakshatra, Tithi, weekday, and planetary positions align favourably — is believed to bring harmony, prosperity, and longevity to the union.

Criteria for Tamil Marriage Muhurta

A muhurta is considered auspicious for marriage when ALL of these conditions are met:

  • Muhurta Nakshatra: Rohini, Mrigashira, Hasta, Chitra, Swati, Anuradha, Mula, Uttara Ashadha, Uttara Bhadrapada, or Revati
  • Shukla Paksha Tithi (waxing Moon): 2, 3, 5, 7, 10, 11, 12, or 13 preferred
  • Auspicious weekday: Monday, Wednesday, Thursday, or Friday
  • No Rahu Kalam during the ceremony hour
  • Jupiter not combust or retrograde (Guru Astra/Vakra)
  • Lagna (rising sign) should be strong — fixed signs (Taurus, Leo, Scorpio, Aquarius) preferred

Months Generally Avoided for Marriage in Tamil Tradition

  • Aadi (July–Aug) — considered inauspicious in many Tamil communities
  • Margazhi (Dec–Jan) — sacred month of Thiruvadhirai / Arudra Darshan
  • Masi (Feb–Mar) — Masi Magam period traditionally avoided
  • Panguni (Mar–Apr) — Panguni Uthiram is actually auspicious, but transition period is avoided
